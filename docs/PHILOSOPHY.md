# The Subtractive Philosophy

## The Core Insight

> "It's easier to know what's NOT needed than what IS needed. Eliminate what shouldn't be done first, and what should be done becomes automatically correct."

Ask anyone "What should your agent do?" and they'll struggle to answer completely. Ask them "What should your agent NEVER do?" and answers come instantly:

- "Never share customer data"
- "Never make promises we can't keep"
- "Never respond in a language we can't support"
- "Never touch billing without approval"

The "no" list is always clearer than the "yes" list. This project leverages that natural human instinct.

## Additive vs Subtractive

| | Additive (Traditional) | Subtractive (Sculpting) |
|---|---|---|
| **Start with** | Nothing | Everything |
| **Process** | Assemble pieces | Remove what's not needed |
| **Risk** | Missing pieces | Over-carving |
| **Security** | Rules that can be broken | Capabilities that don't exist |
| **Feels like** | Engineering | Craftsmanship |
| **Metaphor** | Building a temple from bricks | Carving a temple from a mountain |

## The Ellora Precedent

The Kailasa Temple at Ellora, Maharashtra, India, was carved top-down from a single rock face between the 8th and 9th centuries CE. Approximately 200,000 tonnes of rock were removed.

Key properties of the Ellora approach that apply to our work:

1. **No joints** — The temple is one continuous piece. There are no seams to crack. A sculpted agent has no component boundaries to fail at.

2. **Strength from removal** — The temple's structural integrity comes from what was taken away. A sculpted agent's security comes from capabilities that don't exist.

3. **Vision before tools** — The sculptors saw the temple in the mountain before making the first cut. The sculptor sees the agent before the first carve.

4. **Top-down, not bottom-up** — Start with the whole and refine. Don't start with a foundation and hope it supports what comes next.

## Two Equals

The sculpting process requires both:

**The Sculptor (Human)** — brings vision, creativity, taste, and final authority. The sculptor decides what the agent becomes.

**The Rock (AI)** — brings self-knowledge, capability awareness, and risk warnings. The rock knows what it's made of and warns when a cut is dangerous.

Neither is complete without the other. The sculptor needs the rock. The rock needs the sculptor. The magic lives where they meet.

## Applying This to Code

This philosophy extends to how we build this project:

- **Before adding a dependency, ask:** Is this necessary? Can we do without it?
- **Before adding a feature, ask:** Does this serve the sculptor? Or are we over-engineering?
- **Before adding a file, ask:** Does this earn its existence?
- **Before adding a line of code, ask:** Would the sculpture be weaker without this?

The subtractive philosophy is not minimalism for its own sake. It's intentional removal that reveals what matters.
